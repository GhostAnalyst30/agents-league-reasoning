# Workload and Learning Correlation Report (Synthetic)

> SYNTHETIC DOCUMENT - Fabricated analysis for a fictional organization, aligned to the Work IQ concept of work-context signals.

## Insights

- Employees with **more than 20 meeting hours per week** show a 40% lower study-plan completion rate.
- Optimal completion appears when learners have **12–18 meeting hours** and **at least 15 focus hours** per week.
- Morning learners complete on average 1.4x more scheduled study blocks than evening learners in this fabricated dataset.
- Reminders sent during meeting-heavy windows are dismissed 3x more often than reminders aligned to focus blocks.

## Scheduling Policy

1. Schedule learning blocks **inside focus-heavy periods**, never overlapping recurring meetings.
2. Cap weekly study allocation at 4 hours for capacity-constrained employees (>20 meeting hours/week) and flag them to their manager as at-risk.
3. Always preserve a 5-hour weekly focus buffer that study plans cannot consume.
4. Prefer the learner's `preferred_learning_slot` and `best_days` signals when proposing checkpoints.

## Privacy Note

Work-context signals must be used only in aggregate or for the learner's own plan. Engagement messaging must never reveal another employee's calendar details.
